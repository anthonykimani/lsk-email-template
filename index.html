STATEMENT BY THE LAW SOCIETY OF KENYA ON THE PRESIDENT’S MEMO TO AMEND THE CONSTITUTION

The Council of Law Society of Kenya, led by the president Mr. Eric Theuri, issued a press statement addressing the Memorandum issued by His Excellency the President on the proposed Constitutional Amendments. The Society stated that the proposed amendments are unconstitutional and called upon Kenyans to join the movement as it is our collective duty to uphold the Constitution and rule of Law

LSK notes, with a lot of concern, that leaders in office are proposing constitutional amendments that touch on the root of our democracy and redirecting attention from other pressing national issues.

Full statement: https://bit.ly/LSKStatement

AS THE LAW SOCIETY OF KENYA, WE ARE ASTOUNDED BY THIS MOVE BY THE PRESIDENT GIVEN THAT THE AMENDMENTS WILL AFFECT THE OVERALL STRUCTURE OF PARLIAMENT AND THE EXECUTIVE.

-ERIC THEURI, PRESIDENT OF THE LAW SOCIETY OF KENYA

COURTESY CALL TO THE GOVERNOR OF NAIROBI COUNTY

The Council of the Law Society of Kenya, led by President Eric Theuri, paid a courtesy call to the Governor of Nairobi County, Hon. Johnson Sakaja. The meeting discussed collaboration with the county government on matters to do with ADR, legal and policy reforms among other things.

The Governor reiterated his commitment to pay Advocates pending legal fees, noting that the County has received huge fee notes from advocates. He assured the Society that a committee, including a representative from the Society, would be set up to expedite the matter. He called upon the Society to agree on negotiations on waiving the accumulated interests to enable the County Government to make these payments.

It was also agreed that the LSK will have a Representative to the County Physical & Land Use Planning Liaison & Technical Committee. Further, the Council requested the Governor to consider setting up a Probono Scheme to support young advocates and offer legal aid to the public. Both parties agreed to partner and collaborate in other areas of mutual interest.

CONSULTANCY OPPORTUNITY IN INTERNATIONAL DEVELOPMENT LAW ORGANIZATION (IDLO)

The International Development Law Organization (IDLO) has partnered with Court Annexed Mediation Secretariat and the National AJS Taskforce to support the Judiciary in engaging a consultant to develop court practice direction for the Specialized SGBV Court.

The practice direction will be used to standardize the mediation procedures of the customary justice system for enhanced access to justice, timely and efficient disposal of cases in a gender-inclusive manner, and ensure uniformity in the selection of the mediation and AJS panels and the dispute settlement experiences.
